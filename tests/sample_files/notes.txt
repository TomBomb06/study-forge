Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants convert sunlight into chemical energy. Chlorophyll is the pigment that absorbs light energy. The light-dependent reactions occur in the thylakoid membranes. The Calvin cycle fixes carbon dioxide into glucose using ATP. Cellular respiration releases energy stored in glucose molecules. Mitochondria are the organelles where respiration primarily takes place. Oxygen is produced as a byproduct of photosynthesis and released into air. Stomata are pores in leaves that regulate gas exchange with the atmosphere.
